# Hiring & Interviewing Framework: Building Your Team

**Tier:** Pro  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Target:** Hiring managers, founders, and team leads building or scaling teams

---

## What This Does

A comprehensive framework for hiring—from defining the role to onboarding. Reduces bad hires, improves candidate experience, and builds high-performing teams.

---

## Framework Overview

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks from role definition to offer acceptance

**Phases:**
1. Role definition and planning (Week 1)
2. Sourcing and outreach (Weeks 2-3)
3. Screening and interviewing (Weeks 4-6)
4. Decision and offer (Week 7)
5. Onboarding preparation (Week 8)

---

### Step 1.3: Design Interview Process
**Prompt:**
```
Design the interview process:

# Interview Process: [Role]

## Stage 1: Application Review (15 min per candidate)
**Reviewer:** [Recruiter or hiring manager]
**Criteria:**
- [ ] Meets required skills
- [ ] Relevant experience
- [ ] Clear communication in application
- [ ] Culture fit signals

**Outcome:** Advance to phone screen or reject

---

## Stage 2: Phone Screen (30 min)
**Interviewer:** [Recruiter or hiring manager]
**Goals:**
- Confirm basic qualifications
- Assess communication skills
- Gauge interest and motivation
- Explain role and company

**Questions:**
1. "Walk me through your background" (5 min)
2. "Why are you interested in this role?" (5 min)
3. "Tell me about [relevant experience from resume]" (10 min)
4. "What are you looking for in your next role?" (5 min)
5. Q&A (5 min)

**Scorecard:**
- Communication: [1-5]
- Relevant experience: [1-5]
- Motivation: [1-5]
- Culture fit: [1-5]

**Outcome:** Advance to technical/skills assessment or reject

---

## Stage 3: Skills Assessment (varies)
**Format:** [Take-home project / Live coding / Case study / Portfolio review]
**Time limit:** [Hours or days]
**Evaluator:** [Team member]

**For Technical Roles:**
- Take-home project: [Description]
- Evaluation criteria: [Code quality, problem-solving, communication]

**For Non-Technical Roles:**
- Case study: [Scenario]
- Evaluation criteria: [Thinking process, recommendations, presentation]

**Scorecard:**
- Technical/functional skills: [1-5]
- Problem-solving: [1-5]
- Communication: [1-5]
- Attention to detail: [1-5]

**Outcome:** Advance to team interviews or reject

---

## Stage 4: Team Interviews (3-4 hours, can be split across days)

**Interview 1: Technical Deep Dive (60 min)**
- Interviewer: [Senior team member]
- Focus: Technical skills, problem-solving
- Questions: [List 5-7 technical questions]

**Interview 2: Collaboration & Communication (45 min)**
- Interviewer: [Peer or cross-functional partner]
- Focus: Teamwork, communication style
- Questions: [Behavioral questions about collaboration]

**Interview 3: Leadership & Ownership (45 min)**
- Interviewer: [Hiring manager]
- Focus: Initiative, accountability, growth mindset
- Questions: [Behavioral questions about ownership]

**Interview 4: Values & Culture (30 min)**
- Interviewer: [Team member or founder]
- Focus: Values alignment, culture fit
- Questions: [Questions tied to company values]

**Scorecard (each interviewer completes):**
- Technical/functional skills: [1-5]
- Collaboration: [1-5]
- Ownership: [1-5]
- Culture fit: [1-5]
- Overall recommendation: [Strong yes / Yes / Maybe / No / Strong no]

**Outcome:** Advance to final interview or reject

---

## Stage 5: Final Interview (30-45 min)
**Interviewer:** [Hiring manager or executive]
**Goals:**
- Sell the opportunity
- Address any concerns
- Discuss compensation expectations
- Assess mutual fit

**Questions:**
1. "What questions do you have for me?"
2. "What excites you most about this role?"
3. "What concerns do you have?"
4. "What are your compensation expectations?"
5. "What's your timeline for making a decision?"

**Outcome:** Make offer or reject

---

## Timeline
- Application to phone screen: 3-5 days
- Phone screen to skills assessment: 2-3 days
- Skills assessment to team interviews: 5-7 days
- Team interviews to final interview: 2-3 days
- Final interview to offer: 1-2 days
- **Total:** 2-3 weeks from application to offer

## Candidate Experience
**Communication:**
- Acknowledge application within 24 hours
- Provide feedback at each stage (even rejections)
- Set clear expectations on timeline
- Be responsive to questions

**Respect their time:**
- Consolidate interviews when possible
- Provide interview prep materials
- Offer flexible scheduling
- Pay for take-home projects (if >4 hours)
```

**Decision Point:** Test the interview process with 1-2 candidates and iterate before scaling.

## Phase 3: Interviewing (Weeks 4-6)

### Step 3.1: Interview Question Bank
**Prompt:**
```
Create interview question bank:

## Technical/Functional Questions (Role-Specific)

**For Engineering:**
1. "Walk me through how you'd design [system]"
2. "Tell me about a technical challenge you solved"
3. "How do you approach code review?"
4. "Explain [technical concept] to a non-technical person"

**For Product:**
1. "How do you prioritize features?"
2. "Walk me through a product you shipped"
3. "How do you handle conflicting stakeholder requests?"
4. "Tell me about a time you used data to make a decision"

**For Marketing:**
1. "How do you measure campaign success?"
2. "Walk me through a campaign you ran"
3. "How do you think about positioning?"
4. "Tell me about a time you had to pivot strategy"

**For Sales:**
1. "Walk me through your sales process"
2. "How do you handle objections?"
3. "Tell me about your biggest deal"
4. "How do you build relationships with prospects?"

---

## Behavioral Questions (Universal)

**Collaboration:**
1. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate. How did you handle it?"
2. "Describe a project where you had to work with difficult stakeholders"
3. "How do you give feedback to peers?"

**Ownership:**
1. "Tell me about a time you took initiative on something outside your job description"
2. "Describe a project that failed. What did you learn?"
3. "How do you handle ambiguity?"

**Growth Mindset:**
1. "Tell me about a time you received critical feedback. How did you respond?"
2. "What's something you're currently learning?"
3. "Describe a skill you developed in the last year"

**Problem-Solving:**
1. "Walk me through a complex problem you solved"
2. "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information"
3. "How do you approach learning new things?"

**Leadership (for senior roles):**
1. "Tell me about a time you mentored someone"
2. "How do you build team culture?"
3. "Describe your management philosophy"

---

## Values-Based Questions (Customize to Your Values)

**If you value "Customer Obsession":**
"Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer"

**If you value "Bias for Action":**
"Describe a situation where you had to move fast with imperfect information"

**If you value "Transparency":**
"Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news"

---

## Red Flag Questions (To Probe Concerns)

**Job hopping:**
"I see you've changed jobs frequently. Walk me through your career decisions"

**Gaps in resume:**
"I noticed a gap in [timeframe]. What were you doing during that time?"

**Lack of specific examples:**
"Can you give me a specific example of when you did [skill]?"

**Blaming others:**
"It sounds like your team was the problem. What was your role in that situation?"
```

## Phase 4: Decision & Offer (Week 7)

### Step 4.1: Debrief Meeting
**Prompt:**
```
Conduct hiring debrief:

# Hiring Debrief: [Candidate Name]

**Attendees:** [All interviewers]
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Goal:** Make hire/no-hire decision

---

## Agenda

**1. Round-robin feedback (15 min)**
Each interviewer shares:
- Overall recommendation (Strong Yes / Yes / Maybe / No / Strong No)
- Top 2 strengths
- Top 2 concerns
- Key evidence

**Rules:**
- Hiring manager speaks last (avoid anchoring)
- Focus on evidence, not gut feelings
- Call out biases if you see them

---

**2. Discussion (10 min)**
- Address any concerns
- Probe "Maybe" votes
- Discuss how concerns could be mitigated

---

**3. Decision (5 min)**
- [ ] Make offer
- [ ] Reject
- [ ] Need more information (schedule additional interview)

**If making offer:**
- Compensation: [Amount]
- Start date: [Target]
- Who extends offer: [Name]
- Timeline: [When to send offer]

---

## Decision Criteria

**Minimum bar:**
- All "must-have" skills demonstrated
- Average score of 3.5+ across all criteria
- No "Strong No" votes
- No unresolved red flags

**Ideal candidate:**
- Average score of 4+ across all criteria
- Multiple "Strong Yes" votes
- Clear strengths that match role needs
- Culture add (not just culture fit)

---

## Common Biases to Watch For

**Affinity bias:** Liking someone because they're similar to you
**Halo effect:** One strong trait overshadowing weaknesses
**Horn effect:** One weakness overshadowing strengths
**Recency bias:** Overweighting recent candidates
**Confirmation bias:** Seeking evidence that confirms initial impression

**Mitigation:** Use structured scorecards, focus on evidence, diverse interview panel
```

## Quality Standards

✅ **Success Criteria:**
- Time to hire: <6 weeks from posting to offer acceptance
- Offer acceptance rate: >80%
- New hire satisfaction: 4+ out of 5 in first 90 days
- 90-day retention: >95%
- Hiring manager satisfaction: 4+ out of 5
- Diverse candidate pool: Reflects target demographics
- Candidate experience: Positive feedback even from rejected candidates

❌ **Failure Modes:**
- Hiring too fast (lowering the bar)
- Hiring too slow (losing great candidates)
- Inconsistent interview process (unfair evaluations)
- Poor candidate communication (ghosting)
- Overselling the role (misaligned expectations)
- Ignoring red flags (bad hires)
- No onboarding plan (new hire struggles)

## Usage Notes

- **Hiring bar:** Never lower the bar to fill a role faster
- **Diversity:** Actively work to build diverse teams
- **Candidate experience:** Treat every candidate with respect
- **Feedback:** Always provide feedback to candidates (even rejections)
- **Continuous improvement:** Review and refine process after each hire
- **Team involvement:** Hiring is a team sport, not just the manager's job
